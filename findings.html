Findings
Three things the data showed us that we did not know going in

What we did not know going in

We started this project asking four questions: where do Europeans travel, when, how, and how much. By the end the data had answered most of them. But three things genuinely surprised us. Each is supported by a specific chart in the preceding chapters, and each is the kind of pattern that the raw spreadsheets never reveal at a glance.

Finding 1 of 3

The pandemic permanently rewrote who visits Switzerland.

Before 2020, Germany was the top foreign visitor source for the largest number of Swiss cantons. Americans led only two. By 2024, the United States had become the largest single foreign source for five of the country's biggest tourism hubs: Zurich, Bern, Geneva, Valais and Lucerne. The shift to Zurich, Bern and Valais happened in 2023, two years after travel resumed.

Recovery itself is bifurcated. Geneva, Aargau and Neuchatel grew arrivals by roughly +20% from 2019 to 2024. At the other end, Obwalden lost 21%, Uri 15%, Zug 13%. The cantons that overshot are urban. The ones that stagnated are small alpine resorts. Whatever Switzerland was before 2020, it is not the same destination now.

Evidence: Top foreign origin per canton and 2019 to 2024 recovery on the Switzerland page.
Finding 2 of 3

Tourism is a power law at every scale of zoom.

Across Europe, the top seven destinations absorb the vast majority of all tourist nights. Zoom inside any one country and the picture repeats: Veneto carries Italy, the south carries France, the Alpine arc carries Austria. Most of the rest sits comparatively dark on the map.

Switzerland is the same shape one zoom level deeper. Five of 26 cantons absorb most of the country's arrivals. Inside the calendar, two months absorb most of those arrivals. Inside each canton, a few origin countries send the bulk of the foreign visitors. The structure is not a uniform distribution at any level - it is concentration repeated fractally.

Evidence: Where Europe travels (Trends, NUTS regions) and Where in Switzerland (Canton volumes).
Finding 3 of 3

Proximity wins. Wealth, mostly, does not.

The origin map across Europe fills almost entirely with neighbors. The further away a country sits, the rarer it appears as a top source. Switzerland mirrors this: Germany alone sends over 1.5 million arrivals from roughly 450 km away; beyond 1,500 km the numbers collapse to near zero.

Wealth is a much weaker predictor. Norway, Ireland and Luxembourg sit among Europe's highest GDP per capita (PPS) yet send relatively few visitors to Switzerland. The one consistent counter-example is the United Kingdom - over 1,000 km away yet sending hundreds of thousands of visitors. The pattern suggests that cultural ties and established travel habits may matter more than income alone.

Evidence: Origin maps on the Europe page; Distance scatter and GDP-versus-visits on the Switzerland page.

What future research could explore

The exploration does not end here. The data still contains many patterns and questions worth exploring further.

  • Inbound transport, stays, accommodation Future analysis could examine whether trips to Switzerland differ from other European destinations in transport mode, stay duration and accommodation choice.
  • Visitor demographics and trip purpose by canton Canton-level tourism could further be analyzed and compared through visitor age, income and business-versus-leisure travel patterns.
  • Non-COVID shocks The 2022 Russia-Ukraine war is visible in the data as a collapse in Russian and Ukrainian visitor numbers. Further research could pair tourism data with geopolitical timelines.

Method, sources, team

EUFlow is built entirely on public statistical data. Every chart loads its data directly from CSV or JSON files in the repository. The data went through a preprocessing pipeline and was rendered in the browser with D3.js.

  • Data sources Eurostat tourism datasets (tour_dem_tttr, tour_dem_ttw, tour_dem_tnws, tour_dem_tnac, tour_dem_extotw, tour_occ_nim, tour_occ_nin2c, tour_occ_nin2m), each downloaded directly from ec.europa.eu/eurostat. Swiss Federal Statistical Office PXweb cube px-x-1003020000_102 for canton-by-month-and-year-by-origin arrivals. Eurostat sdg_10_10 (Purchasing power adjusted GDP per capita) for GDP in purchasing power standards. NUTS 2 GISCO geometry for regional maps, world-atlas TopoJSON for country shapes, and the REST Countries API for calculating distances between countries and Switzerland.
  • Preprocessing Raw Eurostat tables were reshaped from wide to long, encoded dimension columns were split into separate variables, missing values handled, and country codes standardised. The FSO 5-dimensional cube was processed and reshaped into compact JSON files used by the Switzerland page.
  • Visual stack Custom charts are built with D3.js, with Plotly used for the destination trends line chart on the Europe page. TopoJSON provides geometry for countries and NUTS regions.
