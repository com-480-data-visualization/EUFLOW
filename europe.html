Tourism in Europe
Explore where, when, and how much Europeans travel across desinations
1

Destination Popularity

Origin

Who sends the most tourists to each destination?

What you see

Each EU country is colored by the country that supplies the most tourist nights for the selected year.

The dropdown lets you scrub through years from 2010 onward. Watch how dominant origin countries spread across neighbors.

Germany shows up as origin across much of central Europe, Spain dominates Portugal, and several Mediterranean and northern countries are driven mainly by their own domestic travelers.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_tttr

Transport

How do Europeans get to their destination?

What you see

For each EU country (rows) and each year (columns), the cell shows the dominant transport mode used by outbound tourists.

Countries are sorted by their most common mode, which forms clean horizontal bands. Row labels carry the icon of the dominant mode for quick scanning.

Air travel takes the lead in island and far flung destinations. Road and car travel prevails in continental Europe. Rail and sea are the favorites of a small minority.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_ttw

Stays

Do Europeans prefer short or long stays?

What you see

A diverging bar centered on 50 percent. The orange portion on the left counts the years between 2012 and 2024 when short stays of 1 to 3 nights were the dominant trip type. The green portion on the right counts the years dominated by long stays of 4 or more nights.

Countries are sorted top to bottom from most long stay dominated to most short stay dominated.

Mediterranean beach countries lean toward long stays. City destinations and central Europe favor short breaks.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_tnws

Accommodation

Where do Europeans sleep when they travel?

What you see

A 100 percent stacked area chart showing how the mix of accommodation types has evolved across Europe between 2012 and 2024.

Each colored band represents a category. Bands are normalized so their sum is always 100 percent. Direct labels on the right give the final 2024 share.

Hotels and rented furnished places together account for most nights. Camping and own property holdings remain niche but steady.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_tnac

Trends

Top destinations over time

What you see

Annual total nights spent in each of the seven leading destinations between 2012 and 2024. Each line tracks one country with its name labeled directly at the right end.

Hover anywhere on the chart to compare values across all destinations for that year.

The 2020 dip from the pandemic is sharp and uniform. Most countries returned to pre pandemic levels by 2022 and have grown since.

Source: Eurostat tour_occ_nim

Geography

Nights spent by destination

What you see

A choropleth of total nights spent in each country for the selected year. The color scale is logarithmic so small countries stay visible despite France and Spain dominating in absolute numbers.

Use the dropdown on the top right to switch years. Toggle the Per capita box to divide each country's total by its population. Croatia, Cyprus and Malta jump up the scale when you switch to per capita.

Hover any country to see the precise value and, in per capita mode, both the rate and the absolute total.

Source: Eurostat tour_occ_nin2c

2

Seasonality

Country view

Top month by country

What you see

Each EU country is colored by the month that recorded the most tourist nights in the selected year. Use the dropdown to switch between 2020 and 2024.

The pandemic year of 2020 stands out. Several countries peak in February, October or December, when restrictions were briefly lifted. By 2022 the summer pattern is fully back.

The United Kingdom and other non EU countries are shown in light gray as geographic context only.

Source: Eurostat tour_occ_nin2m

Region view

Top month by NUTS 2 region

What you see

The same question as the country map but at NUTS 2 resolution. Hundreds of subnational regions are colored by their busiest month in the selected year.

Patterns the country view hides become visible. Alpine and ski regions peak in February. A handful of Mediterranean and city regions peak in September or October.

Hover any region to see its name and busiest month.

Source: Eurostat tour_occ_nin2m with NUTS 2 GISCO geometry

Intensity

How many nights, where and when?

What you see

Pick a month and a year from the dropdowns at the top right of the map. Every NUTS 2 region is then colored by the total number of tourist nights it recorded that month, on a logarithmic color scale.

Switch to August in any year and the Mediterranean coast and Alpine arc burn bright. Switch to February and only the ski regions stay lit while most of Europe goes dark.

Use this view together with the categorical map above. The map above tells you which month a region peaks in. This map tells you how big the peak actually is.

Source: Eurostat tour_occ_nin2m, aggregated across residence and accommodation

Ranking

Top destination each month

What you see

For every calendar month, the bar shows the country that received the most tourist nights across 2020 to 2024 combined. Bar length is total nights, bar color is the country's brand color.

Summer months belong to France, Spain and Italy. The shoulder months and winter shuffle among Germany, France and Spain, with values an order of magnitude smaller.

Source: derived from Eurostat tour_occ_nin2m

3

Travel Expenditure

Geography

How much do tourists spend per trip?

What you see

EU countries colored by the average expenditure per trip of their residents. Darker shades mean travelers from that country spend more on average per trip.

Use the dropdown to scrub through years and watch the spending recover after the 2020 dip. Hover any country to see the exact figure.

Luxembourg, Belgium and Austria sit near the top. Eastern European countries spend less on average per trip but the gap has narrowed since 2012.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw

Intensity

How much do tourists spend per night?

What you see

Same EU countries as before, but now colored by how much their residents spend on average per night while traveling. Per night spending strips out the influence of trip length.

A country with high per night and low per trip values takes short luxury breaks. A country with low per night and high per trip values takes long budget holidays.

Luxembourg, Denmark and Belgium pay the most per night. Eastern Europe pays the least.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw and tour_dem_tnw

Destination

Where in the world does spending climb?

What you see

Same per night metric, but now from the destination side. Each country is colored by how much travelers spend on average per night while visiting it.

Eurostat also publishes spending for entire regions and totals that have no country shape, like Africa, Asia, the Americas and the whole world. These appear as small colored bars at the bottom of the frame, on the same color scale as the map.

The bars labeled Domestic, All foreign trips and World give the reference levels for the average European traveler.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw_per_dest and tour_dem_tnw_per_dest

Destination

Which world destinations cost the most per trip?

What you see

The same world view, but now showing average spending per trip rather than per night. A trip cost combines what travelers spend each night with how long they stay.

Long haul destinations rise sharply on this map because Europeans visit them for longer. North America and Asia stand out compared to nearby European countries.

The bars at the bottom of the frame give the same reading for regions and totals that have no country shape. Africa, the Americas and Asia each show as a single aggregate bar.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw_per_dest

Scope

Domestic, intra EU or beyond

What you see

Three grouped bars for each country show how much its residents spend on average per trip depending on where they go. Green for domestic, blue for trips within the EU, orange for trips outside the EU.

Trips outside the EU are the most expensive almost everywhere. The gap between domestic and outside EU spending tells you how much extra a foreign holiday costs the traveler.

Countries are sorted by their spending on trips outside the EU.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw split by destination scope

Pattern

Long budget trip or short luxury break?

What you see

One dot per country. The horizontal axis is average spending per night, the vertical axis is average spending per trip. Dashed lines mark implied trip lengths of 4, 7, 10 and 14 nights.

Dots far up and to the right are short luxury trips. Dots up and to the left are long budget trips. The ratio of trip cost to night cost gives the average length of a holiday.

Hover any dot to see the exact figures and the implied number of nights per trip.

Source: Eurostat tour_dem_extotw and tour_dem_tnw
